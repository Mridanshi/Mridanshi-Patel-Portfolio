Overview

Where every event becomes a memory

Bowerbird Events is a professional balloon decoration company serving Calgary, Edri, Corine, Chestermere, and Okotoks. They specialise in turning ordinary moments into extraordinary ones through balloon arches, backdrop installations, shimmer walls, and fully themed setups for every kind of celebration.

The design challenge: create a website that felt as whimsical and warm as the events themselves, while building a booking flow that guided clients from curiosity to confirmed quote without friction.

Homepage hero "Your Story, Our Craft" with full-bleed event photography

The Challenge

Making magic feel accessible

Event decoration businesses often struggle with the same UX problem: clients want to dream big but don't know where to start. The services are diverse, the options are many, and the pricing is non-standard. A flat services page leaves too much to guesswork and guesswork loses customers.

The brief was to design a site that would showcase the visual richness of Bowerbird's work, communicate their warm personality, and guide potential clients through a structured, low-friction path to booking all while feeling inviting and beautifully on-brand.

Process

Research, structure, then aesthetics

My process started with understanding both the business and the customer. What occasions were most common? What decisions did clients need to make before committing? What information gaps were causing drop-off? The answers shaped everything from the site architecture to the quote flow.

Visual Identity

Sage, rose, and celebration

The palette was drawn directly from Bowerbird's event photography the sage greens of organic balloon garlands, the warm dusty roses of shimmer walls, the creamy neutrals of their signature arch backdrops. The result is a colour system that feels intrinsically connected to the product itself.

Standout Feature

The guided quote flow

The most ambitious part of this project was the multi-step quote wizard a guided booking experience that breaks a complex decision into simple, delightful steps. Rather than confronting clients with a long form, each screen focuses on exactly one decision at a time.

Step 1 What are we celebrating?
Occasion selection with rich photo tiles: Baby Shower, Birthday, Bridal, Corporate Party, Housewarming, and more. Visual-first to help clients self-identify quickly.
Step 2 Choose a level
Three package tiers presented as cards with real photography, clear pricing, and included features making comparison effortless.
Step 3 Select a theme
Themed icon selector (Bohemian, Jungle, Baby Shower, Mermaid, Astronaut, and more) so clients can match their event personality before customising further.
Step 4 Choose your colours
A full colour palette grid lets clients mix and match balloon colours to their vision bringing personalisation right into the booking flow.

This approach reduces cognitive load, increases completion rates, and gives Bowerbird far more structured data about client needs before the first conversation. It also makes the booking process feel like part of the celebration not a chore.

Reflections

What I took away

  • Booking flows are UX design problems. The quote wizard required the same structured thinking as any complex product feature user goals, decision sequencing, error prevention, and progressive disclosure all applied.
  • Colour and personality aren't decoration they're function. The sage-and-rose palette wasn't just aesthetic; it built immediate trust with the target audience by feeling consistent with the product they were buying.
  • Photography is content, not just decoration. The entire design was built to let Bowerbird's event photography do the selling. Every layout decision was made in service of showcasing the work beautifully.
  • Local clients benefit enormously from thoughtful UX. Bowerbird didn't need a massive design system they needed one really well-considered flow that made their clients feel cared for from the very first click.
